Travelling to Latvian Republic from an EU country

  • In order to travel to Latvian Republic with a dog, it must be microchipped and having a complete European passport. A dog must be vaccinated against rabies with a valid vaccine at the time of travelling and this information stated on the passport. After the vaccination minimum 21 days must pass.

Travelling to Latvian Republic from a country that is not a member of the EU

  • In order to take a dog from a non-EU country into Latvian Republic, you must enter with your dog through one of Border Points for travelers and present the required documents to the Border Guards.
  • In those cases, in which a European passport is not available, the dog must be accompanied by a zoo sanitary certificate, which must be signed by an official veterinarian from the country of origin and present a declaration, at least in English, both according to the EU rules and a certified copy of the identification and vaccination data of each dog.
  • Additionally, your dog must be identified with a microchip and be vaccinated against rabies with a valid vaccine at the time of travelling. If you arrive from a country not listed in the Annex II of Regulation (EU) 577/2013, your will need to present a serological test against rabies (see section 4) done by an authorized laboratory, for instant, travelers from Ukraine.

Important note

Transportation of up to five dogs is considered for non-commercial purposes. There are exceptions for competitions, exhibitions or sport activities, duly documented for animals older than 6 months under these conditions:

  • Not for a commercial purpose or a transfer of ownership.
  • They travel with their owners or persons in charge of the dog/s in their names during the trip.

Dogs are allowed to travel separated from their owner/handler if there is a maximum of five days between the arrival of the owner/handler and the dog/s.
